Pour Picks

Inside the app

Six things it does, and the specific way it does each one. Laid out in the order you would actually use them, from your first scanned bottle to what the shelf is worth.

  1. 01 Cellar Owned · Wishlist · Finished

    Your cellar, organized

    Every bottle by distillery, expression, proof, age, and fill level. Flag each one Owned, Wishlist, or Finished, and log your rating the moment you set the glass down. Filter and search to see what is open, what is still sealed, and what is on the chase list.

  2. 02 Catalog Bourbon · Rye · Tennessee · Wheat · Malt

    The whole American shelf

    Browse the full American-whiskey catalog. Filter by distillery, age, proof, or price, or widen to world spirits when you want to look past Kentucky. Every entry carries its mash bill, flavor tags, and food and drink pairings, with a path straight back to your cellar.

  3. 03 Pour Concierge

    Scan the label, get the bottle

    Named and matched, usually in under a second.

    Point your camera at the front label. Pour Concierge reads it with Claude Vision, names the distillery, expression, and proof, and matches it to the catalog. If it misses, describe the bottle in words, search by hand, or add it yourself. Every add carries a 4.5-second undo, so nothing lands in your cellar by accident.

  4. 04 Palate 3 questions, or 9

    Find your next pour

    Answer three quick questions, or nine if you want to go deep. The quiz maps what you already like to bottles you have not tried yet. Stick to Classics leans on the icons; Surprise Me pushes the deep cuts forward.

  5. 05 Journal Nose · Palate · Finish

    A journal that remembers

    Log every pour with one overall rating plus separate marks for nose, palate, and finish. Note how you took it (neat, rocks, water, cocktail), the pour size, and whatever you want to say in your own words. Your aggregate rating shows on the bottle so other collectors know whether to chase it.

  6. 06 Value MSRP, or what you paid

    What the shelf is worth

    Cellar value updates with every bottle. MSRP is the default; override it with what you actually paid. Per-bottle and per-line totals, with steppers for the ones you own more than one of.
