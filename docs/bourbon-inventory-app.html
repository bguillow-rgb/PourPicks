Quick Answer

A bourbon inventory app is a phone app that catalogs every bottle in your collection, distillery, expression, proof, fill level, and value, so you always know what you own. Pour Picks does this on iOS: scan a label to add a bottle, track collection value, and log every pour in one private cellar.

Why use an app to track your bourbon collection?

A growing bourbon collection quickly outgrows a spreadsheet or a notes file. You forget which bottles are open, buy a duplicate you already own, or lose track of what a rare pour is now worth. A dedicated bourbon inventory app keeps every bottle in one place, searchable, valued, and backed up to your account.

Pour Picks was built for exactly this. Instead of typing out each bottle, you scan the label and the app fills in the distillery, expression, and proof for you. It is the difference between a chore you stop doing and a cellar you actually keep current.

How do you add bottles to your cellar?

Point your iPhone camera at the front label. The Pour Concierge scanner uses Claude Vision to identify the distillery, expression, and proof, then matches against a catalog of thousands of American whiskeys, usually in under a second. No manual data entry for the common case.

When a bottle is missing or the scan isn't right, you can describe it in words for a second pass, search the catalog by name, or suggest a new bottle to be added. An undo snackbar gives you 4.5 seconds to reverse any add, so mistakes never stick.

Can it track how much my collection is worth?

Yes, collection value updates with every bottle you add. Pour Picks uses catalog MSRP as the default price and lets you override it with what you actually paid. You get per-bottle and per-line totals, plus quantity steppers for the bottles you own in multiples.

Mark each bottle Owned, Wishlist, or Finished, and the running total reflects only what's actually resting in your cellar. It's a real number for insurance, resale decisions, or just knowing where your hobby budget went.

Does it remember your tasting notes?

Every pour can be logged with an overall rating, separate nose, palate, and finish ratings, how you served it (neat, rocks, water, or cocktail), pour size, and freeform notes. Edit and revisit any entry later, so your tasting history grows alongside your inventory.

A "Pour Tonight" feature reads your actual cellar and suggests a specific bottle to open tonight, based on what you've poured recently, your ratings, and the season, a recommendation from what you already own, not a prompt to buy more.
